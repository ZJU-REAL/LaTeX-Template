\subsection{Recommended Section Structure}
\label{sec:sections}

A standard academic paper should include:

\begin{enumerate}[topsep=6pt, itemsep=4pt]
    \item \textbf{Introduction} ---Motivation, problem statement, contributions
    \item \textbf{Related Work} ---Existing methods, relationship to your work
    \item \textbf{Background / Preliminaries} ---Notation, definitions, foundational concepts
    \item \textbf{Method / Approach} ---Core technical contribution
    \item \textbf{Experiments} ---Datasets, baselines, results, analysis
    \item \textbf{Conclusion} ---Summary, limitations, future work
\end{enumerate}

\begin{definition}[Normal Distribution]
\label{def:normal}
A continuous random variable $X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)$ has probability density function:
\begin{equation}
    f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}} \exp\!\Big(-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\Big).
    \label{eq:normal_pdf}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Pythagorean Identity]
\label{lm:pythagorean}
For any orthogonal vectors $\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b} \in \mathbb{R}^n$:
\begin{equation}
    \|\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}\|^2 = \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 + \|\mathbf{b}\|^2.
    \label{eq:pythagorean}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The proof follows from the definition of inner product and orthogonality.
\end{proof}

Reference equations with \verb|\eqref|: e.g., Equation~\eqref{eq:normal_pdf}.

    \ENSURE Updated parameters $\theta$
    \STATE Initialize $\theta \gets \theta_0$
    \FOR{epoch $ = 1$ to $T$}
      \STATE $\mathcal{L} \gets 0$
      \FOR{each batch $B$}
        \STATE $\mathcal{L} \gets \mathcal{L} + \ell(f_\theta(x), y)$

\subsection{Equations and Math}
\label{sec:equations}

Inline math: $\|\mathbf{x}\|_2^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i^2$.

Display equations:

\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L}(\theta) = -\mathbb{E}_{x\sim p_{\text{data}}}\big[\log D_\theta(x)\big]
    - \mathbb{E}_{z\sim p_z}\big[\log(1-D_\theta(G_\phi(z)))\big].
    \label{eq:gan_loss}
\end{equation}

Aligned equations:

\begin{align}
    \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \theta}
        &= \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial f} \cdot \frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta} \\
        &= \nabla_f \mathcal{L} \cdot J_f.
    \label{eq:chain_rule}
\end{align}

\subsection{Purpose and Structure}
\label{sec:rw-purpose}

The Related Work section serves three goals:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Show your knowledge} of the field and relevant prior work
    \item \textbf{Position your work} relative to existing approaches
    \item \textbf{Highlight novelty} by clearly stating differences from prior work
\end{enumerate}

Organize related work \textbf{by topic or approach}, not by chronology. Group methods that share similar ideas, assumptions, or techniques together.

\subsection{Common Organization Patterns}
\label{sec:rw-patterns}

\subsection{Template Sentences}
\label{sec:rw-templates}

Use these templates for smooth transitions:

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Summarizing a body of work:}

\begin{promptbox}{Templates}
\begin{verbatim}
% General area
Recently, there has been growing interest in ... ~\citep{}.

% Gap identification
While these methods achieve strong performance on ..., they largely overlook ...

% Prior survey
\citet{smith2020} provide a comprehensive survey on ...
\end{verbatim}
\end{promptbox}

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Discussing a specific prior work:}

\begin{promptbox}{Templates}
\begin{verbatim}
% Basic summary
\citet{smith2020} propose a framework for ...
Their approach leverages ... to achieve ...

% Method details
Given an input $x$, their method first computes ...
Then, it applies ... to produce the final output.

% Limitation
However, this approach assumes ..., which may not hold in ...
\end{verbatim}
\end{promptbox}

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Positioning your work:}

\begin{promptbox}{Templates}
\begin{verbatim}
% Contrast with prior work
Unlike \citet{smith2020}, which relies on ...,
our method directly optimizes ...

% Extension of prior work
Building on the insight of \citet{smith2020},
we further propose ...

% Complementary to prior work
While \citet{smith2020} focuses on accuracy, our work
specifically addresses efficiency and ...

% Key difference
The key distinction of our approach is ...
\end{verbatim}
\end{promptbox}

\subsection{Related Work Checklist}
\label{sec:rw-checklist}

Before finalizing, verify:

\begin{itemize}
    \item[] $\square$ Have you cited all relevant prior work?
    \item[] $\square$ Are citations accurate and fairly represent the methods?
    \item[] $\square$ Is the organization logical (by topic, not chronologically)?
    \item[] $\square$ Does each subsection have a brief introduction?
    \item[] $\square$ Have you clearly positioned your work relative to others?
    \item[] $\square$ Are you missing any recent, important references?
    \item[] $\square$ Is the writing tone neutral and objective?
\end{itemize}
